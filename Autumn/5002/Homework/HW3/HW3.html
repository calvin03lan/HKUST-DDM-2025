Q2¶

(4 marks)¶

The 2nd derivatives of a multivariable scalar function $f(x_1, x_2, ..., x_n)$ can be packed into a matrix form called Hessian matrix $\mathcal{H}$, which looks like:

$$ \mathcal{H}(x_1, x_2,...,x_n) = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_1^2} & \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_1 \partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_1\partial x_n} \\ \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_2 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_2^2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_2 \partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_n \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_n \partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial^2f}{\partial x_n^2} \\ \end{bmatrix} $$

Write a function H() that can compute the Hessian matrix of the function

$$f(x_1, x_2, ..., x_n) = e^{x_1^2+x_2^2+...+x_n^2}$$

such that the result is returned in form of a NumPy array. The function is required to be able to determine $n$ automatically by how many inputs the user is giving to H(), e.g. if the user calls H(x1,x2), the output should be a 2x2 matrix; if the user calls H(x1,x2,x3,x4), the output should be a 4x4 matrix.

Bonus: Construct the matrix entirely with NumPy broadcasting. No for loop, while loop or if-else clause in the whole program.

Q4¶

A farmer is growing a kind of crop in a 200 $\times$ 200 rectangular field. The field can be considered as a grid, with 1 crop in each cell. The growth of the crop follows these rules:

  • Initially (Day 0), its seed is buried underground and the crop's height can be considered as 0.
  • Starting from day 1, the crop will grow a (uniformly) random height between 0.01-0.04 units every day.
  • When the crop is taller than or equal to 5 units, it will stop growing.
  • The crop can grow 1 fruit when it is taller than or equal to 2 units, and 1 extra fruit for each subsequent unit. i.e. The crop will have 1 fruit if its height is between 2 to 3 units, 2 fruits if its height is between 3 to 4 units, and so on.

After a certain number of days, The farmer will harvest and re-plant the whole field (reset to height 0). The yield of the harvest is counted by the number of fruit the farmer can collect.

(Part a, 3 marks)¶

We want to find out what is the optimal frequency the farmer should harvest the crop. Although by waiting until every crop reaching 5-tall will give the highest yield per harvest, this is not the best long term strategy. This is because one may get more fruits on average by harvesting and re-planting more frequently within a fixed duration. We should use a better measure like yield per planted day $=\frac{\text{yield if harvest on day X}}{\text{X}}$ to determine the best frequency.
